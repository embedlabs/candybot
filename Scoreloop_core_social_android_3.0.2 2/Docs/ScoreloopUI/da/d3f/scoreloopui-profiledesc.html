User Profiles

Q. What is meant by a user?
A user refers to a game-player, the end-user of your game application.

Q. What happens the first time I connect to Scoreloop?
The first time a user connects to Scoreloop they are assigned a unique identifier that is used in all subsequent connections.
New users also receive an automatically generated username, (which has the form player_XXXX). Users are encouraged to change this to something more personal, (see the guide to the Scoreloop Entry Screen for details about how to implement this.

Q. How do I login to Scoreloop?
Logging into Scoreloop is an automatic process that happens whenever a user playing a Scoreloop-enabled game makes contact with our servers.
There is no explicit step where users are required to enter their username or password.

Q. What information is contained in my user profile?
Your user profile contains, (at a minimum), your Scoreloop username. This will either be the username automatically generated for you by Scoreloop the first time you connected, or else a username you have chosen for yourself. Your profile may also optionally contain your email address as well as an image. Your profile may also have an associated list of designated Scoreloop friends, (your 'buddy-list').

Q. Can I change the information in my profile?
Yes. You are free to change or update your username and email. Read the guide to the Scoreloop Entry Screen to see how to do this.

Q. What is meant by the term 'session user'?
This is the user associated with the device that is currently connected to Scoreloop.

Q. Can multiple users share the same device ?
No. Multiple users cannot use the same device to connect to Scoreloop. Every device that is used to play a Scoreloop enabled game is uniquely associated with a single specific user.

Q. Can multiple devices share the same User Profile?
Yes, multiple devices can share the same Scoreloop User Profile. If you would like to do this, please get in touch with us via our support forum http://support.scoreloop.com .
