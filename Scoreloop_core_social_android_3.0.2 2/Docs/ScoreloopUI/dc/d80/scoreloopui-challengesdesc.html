Challenges

Q. What is a challenge?
A Scoreloop challenge is where 2 players compete against each other in order to get the best score and win a prize of in-game currency.

Q. Do they compete against each other in real time?
No. In a challenge players do not compete against each other simultaneously. First one player plays the game, gets a score, and submits it to Scoreloop. Then the second player plays the game, gets a score, and submits it to Scoreloop. The player with the best score wins the challenge.

Q. How is the 'best' score determined?
The best score is determined according to how you have configured scoring for your game on https://developer.scoreloop.com .

Q. What is meant by 'contender' and 'contestant'?
These terms have very specific meanings for Scoreloop:

  • The contender is the user who starts the challenge.
  • The contestant is the user who accepts the challenge.

Q. What is the challenge stake?
The stake is the amount of in-game currency that both players must contribute in order to take part in the challenge.

Q. Who decides how big the stake should be?
The contender (that is the player who starts the challenge) decides the size of the stake. The stake options available to the contender are dependent on their balance of in-game currency: the larger the balance, the larger the minimum stake will be. So, for example, a user with a balance of 10 units might be able to place a minimum stake of 1 unit, whereas for a user with a balanc of 100 unit the minimum stake might be 5 units. This calculation happens server-side.

Q. How much is the Challenge Prize ?
The challenge winner gets the challenge 'prize', which is an amount of in-game currency. The challenge prize is calculated depending on the challenge sink that the developer has chosen to use. The challenge sink represents the cost of a challenge, and is calculated as a percentage of the total virtual currency pool of a challenge (the sum of the stakes contributed by each player).

For example: When 25% sink is in place: If both players contribute 5 coins to play a challenge, the winner is awarded 7.5 coins because 2.5 coins (10 - 25% = 2.5) are taken as the challenge cost. When no sink (0%) is in place: If both players contribute 5 coins to play a challenge, the winner is awarded 10 coins (10 - 0% = 10).

The following examples demonstrate the currency wallet during a challenge:

25% challenge sink

  • Player’s balance before challenge: 10 units
  • Challenge stake: 5 units
  • Player’s balance after paying stake (10 - 5): 5 units,
  • Challenge prize: (100-25%) of (2 * stake) = 75% of (2 * 5) = 7.5 units
  • Player’s balance after winning challenge: (balance after paying stake + challenge prize) = (5 + 7.5) = 12.5 units

0% challenge sink

  • Player’s balance before challenge: 10 units
  • Challenge stake: 5 units
  • Player’s balance after paying stake (10 - 5): 5 units,
  • Challenge prize: (100-0%) of (2 * stake) = 100% of (2 * 5) = 10 units
  • Player’s balance after winning challenge: (balance after paying stake + challenge prize) = 15 units

Q. What does the winner of the challenge get?

The player who loses the challenge loses their stake outright. The winner of the challenge receives the challenge prize only, which does not include their stake.

The following example makes this clear:

  1. At the start of the challenge:
    • Contender balance: 100 units
    • Contestant balance: 10 units
  2. The challenge stake is, say, 5 units.
  3. Balances after paying the stake:
    • Contender balance: 95 units
    • Contestant balance: 5 units
  4. The challenge prize: (25% sink) = (100-25%) of (2 * stake) = 75% of (2 * 5) = 7.5 units. (0% sink) = (100-0%) of (2 * stake) = 100% of (2 * 5) = 10 units
  5. if contender == winner:
    • (25% sink)
      • Contender balance = (balance after paying stake) + (prize) = 95 + 7.5 = 102.5 units
      • Contender balance = balance after paying stake = 5 units
    • (0% sink)
      • Contender balance = (balance after paying stake) + (prize) = 95 + 10 = 105 units
      • Contestant balance = balance after paying stake = 5 units
  6. if contestant == winner:
    • (25% sink)
      • Contender balance = (balance after paying stake) = 95
      • Contestant balance = (balance after paying stake) + (prize) = 5 + 7.5 = 12.5 units
    • (0% sink)
      • Contender balance = (balance after paying stake) = 95
      • Contestant balance = (balance after paying stake) + (prize) = 5 + 10 = 15 units

Q. Are there different types of challenges?
There are two types: 'open' and 'direct'. An open challenge is one that can be accepted by anyone. A direct challenge is one that has been assigned to a specific, named Scoreloop user. In a direct challenge only the user to whom the challenge has been assigned may accept it.

Q. Who decides whether a challenge is 'open' or 'direct'?
The contender (the user who starts the challenge) decides whether it is to be open or direct.

Q. Does Scoreloop do anything to try to promote fairness in challenges?
Yes. Scoreloop calculates a 'skill level' for each Scoreloop user. This skill level is dependent on how well a user has performed in past challenges, (how many they have won and lost). The skill level for a user will change over time. When a user requests a list of all open challenges, we only display open challenges from other users with a similar skill level. This is to ensure that new or weaker players aren't unfairly targeted by stronger or more experienced ones.

Q. How can I keep track of what challenges I have taken part in?
The ScoreloopUI Challenges View displays your challenge history, which is a record of challenges played, won, and lost by you.
